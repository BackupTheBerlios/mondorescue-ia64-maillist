Hi Ravi,

 

According to your earlier message to the list, you did get (a different system?) to create an archive, but can’t get it to restore because of the newt problem. More on that shortly.

 

This system you indicate you’re unable to create the archive.

 

I do see in your mindi.log:

WARNING - /bin/bunzip2 does not exist; cannot be LDD'd.

/bin/bzip2

WARNING - /bin/bzip2 does not exist; cannot be LDD'd.

Which you have acknowledged in your earlier post. Be sure you have bzip installed, it is a dependency.

 

But also, in your mondoarchive.log:

tar: dev/cciss/c0d0: time stamp 2011-03-27 23:31:17 is 12342 s in the future

tar: dev/cciss/c0d0p1: time stamp 2011-03-27 23:31:17 is 12342 s in the future

tar: dev/cciss/c0d0p2: time stamp 2011-03-27 23:31:17 is 12342 s in the future

.................................................Unable to handle [extra module file not found]

Everything is moving along fine until you hit that message, and then everything seems to go poorly after that. You may want to check the timestamp of that device file against your system clock, it may be that this check is causing your failure.

 

However, even if you get the archive to create, it seems there is a problem with the recovery on ia64, that you have discovered on your other system. I have seen this problem with ‘newt’ on my systems as well. I’ve been trading messages with Bruno Cornec on this but haven’t seen any update from him lately.

 

Thanks, and good luck!

 

 

Dean Lester
